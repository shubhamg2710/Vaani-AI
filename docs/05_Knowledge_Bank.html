Banking, Financial Services & Insurance — Products, Rates, Regulations, and Customer Guidance

A1

Banking Products & Services

BFSI

Savings Accounts

  • Regular Savings: Min balance Rs.1,000–10,000 (branch-dependent)
  • Zero Balance: SBI Basic Savings Bank Deposit (BSBD), Jan Dhan accounts
  • Salary Account: Zero balance, employer-linked; reverts to regular if salary stops 3 months
  • Senior Citizen Accounts: Higher interest, preferential services
  • Interest Rate: 2.70–4.00% p.a. (SBI 2.70%, HDFC 3.0–3.5%, IDFC First up to 7% on higher balances)
  • Interest calculated daily on end-of-day balance; credited quarterly

Current Accounts

  • Designed for businesses — high transaction volume, no interest paid
  • Overdraft facility available
  • Min balance: Rs.5,000–1,00,000 (varies by bank & tier)
  • Charges for cash deposits beyond free limit (typically 0.3–1% of excess)
  • Types: Regular, Premium, Trade, Super Premium

Fixed Deposits (FD)

  • Tenure: 7 days to 10 years
  • SBI: 6.50–7.50% (general), 7.00–8.00% (senior citizen)
  • HDFC Bank: 6.60–7.75%, SC +0.50%
  • ICICI Bank: 6.70–7.75%, SC +0.25–0.50%
  • TDS deducted if interest > Rs.40,000/year (Rs.50,000 for seniors)
  • Premature withdrawal penalty: 0.50–1.00%
  • Loan against FD: up to 90% of FD value

Recurring Deposits (RD)

  • Fixed monthly installment savings product
  • Minimum deposit: Rs.100/month (SBI), Rs.500/month (HDFC)
  • Tenure: 6 months to 10 years
  • Interest: similar to FD for equivalent tenure
  • Suitable for disciplined monthly savings

Investment Products

  • Mutual Funds: Equity, Debt, Hybrid; SIP from Rs.100–500/month
  • PPF: 15-year lock-in, current rate 7.1% p.a., Section 80C benefit
  • NPS: Pension scheme; tax benefit under 80CCD(1B) up to Rs.50,000 extra
  • ELSS: 3-year lock-in tax-saving MF, 80C deduction
  • SSY: Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana — 8.2% for girl child

Credit Cards

  • Types: Rewards, Cashback, Travel, Fuel, Co-branded (Amazon Pay, Flipkart Axis)
  • Billing cycle: typically 30 days; due date 15–20 days after statement
  • Minimum due: 5% of outstanding (minimum Rs.200)
  • Interest on revolving credit: 2.5–3.5%/month = 30–42% p.a.
  • Credit limit based on income and CIBIL score
  • Late payment fee: Rs.500–1,300 depending on outstanding

Debit Cards

  • Variants: Classic, Platinum, Business Debit, RuPay, Visa, Mastercard
  • Daily ATM withdrawal limit: Rs.20,000–1,00,000 (varies by variant)
  • Daily POS/online limit: Rs.50,000–5,00,000
  • International usage: must be enabled separately (RBI mandate)
  • RuPay: India's own network; accepted on UPI as well
AI Agent Note: When a customer asks about interest rates, always caveat that rates are subject to change and recommend they check the bank's official website or contact the branch for the latest applicable rate. Do NOT guarantee specific rates unless real-time data is integrated.

  • Shishu: Up to Rs.50,000 — micro entrepreneurs
  • Kishore: Rs.50,000 – Rs.5 Lakh — established small businesses
  • Tarun: Rs.5 Lakh – Rs.10 Lakh — growth-stage businesses
  • No collateral required under CGTMSE guarantee cover
  • Applicable for non-farm income-generating activities
  • Apply via Mudra.org.in or any PSB/NBFC

Key Loan Concepts

  • MCLR: Marginal Cost of Funds Based Lending Rate — floating rate benchmark
  • RLLR: Repo Linked Lending Rate — directly linked to RBI repo rate
  • EMI: Equated Monthly Installment = (P × r × (1+r)^n) / ((1+r)^n - 1)
  • Prepayment: Floating rate home loans — no prepayment penalty (RBI rule)
  • Moratorium: EMI holiday during COVID / specific schemes — interest accrues
  • CIBIL Score impact: Missed EMI = immediate negative impact; 30+ days late = significant drop
A3

Insurance Products (Bancassurance)

Life Insurance

  • Term Insurance: Pure protection; Rs.1 Cr cover from ~Rs.700/month (healthy 30-yr male); no maturity benefit
  • ULIPs: Market-linked investment + insurance; 5-year lock-in
  • Endowment Plans: Savings + protection; lower returns but guaranteed
  • Key providers: LIC, HDFC Life, ICICI Prudential, SBI Life, Max Life, Bajaj Allianz
  • Section 80C: premium deduction up to Rs.1.5L; maturity under 10(10D)

Health Insurance

  • Individual: Covers single person; premium based on age & sum insured
  • Family Floater: All family members share one sum insured
  • Critical Illness: Lump sum on diagnosis of listed illnesses (cancer, heart attack)
  • Senior Citizen Plans: Higher premium; pre-existing disease waiting period 2–4 years
  • Cashless at network hospitals; reimbursement for non-network
  • Section 80D: deduction up to Rs.25,000 (Rs.50,000 for seniors)

Motor Insurance

  • Third-Party (TP): Mandatory by law (Motor Vehicles Act); premium fixed by IRDAI
  • Comprehensive: TP + own damage; much higher coverage
  • Key Add-ons: Zero Depreciation, Engine Protect, RSA (Roadside Assistance), NCB Protect, Invoice Value
  • NCB: 20% (Year 1) → 25% → 35% → 45% → 50% (Year 5+)
  • IDV: Insured Declared Value = market value at time of policy

Government Schemes

  • PMJJBY: Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana — Rs.436/year → Rs.2 Lakh life cover (age 18–50)
  • PMSBY: Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana — Rs.20/year → Rs.2 Lakh accident cover (age 18–70)
  • PM Fasal Bima Yojana: Crop insurance for farmers
  • Both PMJJBY & PMSBY: auto-debit from savings account; renew annually
  • Home Insurance: structure (RCC construction) + contents separately insurable
Regulatory Note: IRDAI Ombudsman handles insurance complaints. Customers can approach Bima Bharosa portal (IRDAI's integrated grievance platform). Complaint must be filed within 1 year of insurer's final reply.

Digital Banking

Internet Banking

  • Fund transfers: NEFT, RTGS, IMPS via net banking
  • Security: OTP on registered mobile, 2FA mandatory for transactions
  • Limits: RTGS min Rs.2 Lakh; self-account transfers typically unlimited
  • Common features: statement download, FD booking, loan repayment, tax payment

Mobile Banking Apps

  • SBI YONO: Full banking + marketplace; 7 Cr+ downloads
  • HDFC Mobile Banking: Best UI ratings; PayZapp wallet integrated
  • ICICI iMobile Pay: Open to non-ICICI customers for UPI
  • Kotak 811: Zero-balance digital account via app
  • MPIN / biometric authentication for quick access

UPI (Unified Payments Interface)

  • Operates 24x7x365 including holidays
  • Standard daily limit: Rs.1 Lakh per transaction/day; Rs.2 Lakh for verified users
  • Apps: BHIM (NPCI), Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, Amazon Pay
  • UPI Lite: Offline small payments up to Rs.500/txn, Rs.2,000 wallet limit
  • UPI 123Pay: for feature phone users via IVR/missed call
  • UPI One World: for foreign nationals in India

Fund Transfer Methods

  • NEFT: Batch processing (every 30 min); free post Jan 2020 (RBI mandate); no min/max
  • RTGS: Real-time gross settlement; minimum Rs.2 Lakh; for large value; available 7am–6pm weekdays
  • IMPS: Instant 24x7; Rs.1–5 Lakh limit; charges Rs.2.50–25 (bank-dependent)
  • Cheque / CTS: CTS clearing — same-day for local; 2 working days for outstation

A5

Regulatory & Compliance (RBI / IRDAI)

KYC Requirements

  • Officially Valid Documents (OVD): Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID, Driving License, NREGA card
  • Aadhaar + PAN is the most common combination
  • Video KYC (V-CIP): RBI-approved digital KYC; no branch visit needed
  • Periodic Re-KYC: every 2 years for high-risk, 8 years for low-risk customers
  • CKYC Registry (CERSAI) — single KYC valid across financial institutions

CIBIL Score

  • Range: 300 (poorest) to 900 (best)
  • 750+ = excellent; 700–749 = good; 650–699 = fair; <650 = poor
  • Payment History: 35% weightage (most critical)
  • Credit Utilization: 30% (keep below 30% of limit)
  • Length of Credit History: 15%
  • Credit Mix: 10% (secured + unsecured blend)
  • New Inquiries: 10% (each hard inquiry reduces score)

Grievance Redressal

  • Level 1: Bank's own complaint system (branch / toll-free / app)
  • Level 2: RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme (free of cost) — cms.rbi.org.in
  • Ombudsman: file after 30 days of unresolved bank complaint
  • IRDAI Bima Bharosa: Insurance grievances — igms.irda.gov.in
  • Consumer Forum: COPA for banking + insurance disputes > Rs.1 Cr

Card Security & Data Privacy

  • Card Tokenization: RBI mandate effective Oct 2022 — merchants cannot store raw card data; use tokens instead
  • Card-not-present transactions: AFA (2FA/OTP) mandatory for amounts >Rs.2,000
  • RBI Data Localization: Payment data must be stored in India
  • DPDP Act 2023: Digital Personal Data Protection — explicit consent, data minimization, right to erasure
  • SARFAESI Act: bank empowered to recover NPA secured assets without court intervention
For AI Agent: If a customer reports unauthorized transaction, the immediate response should be: (1) Ask them to block the card immediately via bank app or helpline, (2) Note down the transaction details, (3) File dispute within 3 working days for maximum zero-liability protection under RBI circular. Zero liability applies if customer reports within 3 days.
A6

Common Queries & Suggested AI Responses

Q: How do I update my mobile number in my bank account?
A: You can update your registered mobile number through: (1) Bank branch — bring original Aadhaar + PAN, fill mobile update form; most banks process same day. (2) ATM — some banks (SBI, HDFC) allow via ATM with debit card + PIN. (3) Net banking / app — available if OTP can be received on old number. Note: if old number is completely inaccessible, branch visit is mandatory. The update is typically processed within 1–2 working days.
Q: How do I generate / download my account statement?
A: Options: (1) Net banking / Mobile app — log in → Accounts → Statement → select date range → download PDF. (2) Email statement — request via app or SMS (e.g., SBI: send "ESTMT <Account Number> <month> <year>" to 9223588888). (3) Passbook — update at branch or passbook printer kiosk. (4) ATM — mini-statement (last 5–10 transactions). For home loan/FD interest statements for tax purposes, use the annual interest certificate option in net banking.
Q: My debit/credit card is lost or stolen. What should I do?
A: Act immediately: (1) Block card instantly via bank's mobile app (Cards section → Block/Hot-list) or call 24x7 toll-free number. (2) Report to bank in writing within 24 hours for liability protection. (3) Request replacement card — typically delivered in 5–7 working days. (4) If fraudulent transactions already occurred, dispute them immediately. Under RBI circular, if reported within 3 days, zero liability for customer if it's third-party fraud. Keep all transaction details handy.
Q: How do I stop an ECS / NACH mandate (auto-debit)?
A: Two ways: (1) Through the originator (lender/institution who set up the mandate) — submit cancellation request minimum 15 days before next debit date. (2) Through your bank — NACH mandate cancellation request at branch or via net banking (Payments → Manage Mandates → Cancel). Effective from next cycle. Note: EMI mandates for active loans — contact lender first; cancellation doesn't waive the EMI. Keep cancellation acknowledgment.
Q: How do I find my bank's IFSC code / SWIFT code?
A: IFSC (for domestic transfers): (1) Printed on first page of cheque book, (2) Bank's website branch locator, (3) RBI website: rbi.org.in/Scripts/IFSCMICRDetails.aspx. Format: 4-letter bank code + 0 + 6-digit branch code (e.g., SBIN0001234). SWIFT code (for international transfers): 8–11 character code; check bank's website under international banking section. SBI example: SBININBB. Branch-specific SWIFT codes differ — contact international banking division for exact code.
Q: How do I update the nominee in my account / FD?
A: RBI has mandated simplified nominee process from January 2024. (1) Net banking: Services → Nomination → Add/Change nominee → enter nominee name, relation, DOB, address, share %. (2) Branch: Form DA1 (for accounts), Form 2 (for lockers). (3) Up to 4 nominees now allowed for bank accounts (as per recent amendment). Nomination does not affect legal heirship — it's for bank to facilitate handover; legal heirs may contest.
Q: How do I close my bank account?
A: Steps: (1) Ensure zero balance and no pending mandates/standing instructions. (2) Visit home branch with original passbook, debit card (to be surrendered), unused cheque leaves. (3) Fill account closure form. (4) If balance exists, it will be paid via DD or transfer to another account. Charges: Closure within 14 days — free. 14 days to 12 months — Rs.100–500 at most banks. After 1 year — usually free. Note: Joint account requires signatures from all holders.

Common Vehicle Complaints & Resolution Guidance

Engine Issues

  • Metal knocking / heavy thumping: SERIOUS — advise customer to stop driving immediately; tow to service center. Could be con-rod bearing failure
  • Ticking / tapping noise: Often valve clearance issue or low oil level. Check oil immediately. Likely not roadside emergency but needs service
  • Overheating: Check coolant level, thermostat, radiator blockage. Stop vehicle if temperature gauge in red
  • Excessive oil consumption: Valve seal wear or piston ring issue; needs dealer inspection
  • Hard starting: Battery, fuel pump, or spark plug issue depending on pattern

Electrical Issues

  • Battery drain overnight: Likely alternator not charging or parasitic draw (aftermarket accessory); test with multimeter
  • AC not cooling: Most common cause — refrigerant recharge needed (Rs.800–2,000 typically); could also be compressor clutch or condenser issue
  • Infotainment crash / freeze: Usually firmware bug; software update from service center resolves; factory reset as first step
  • Check Engine light: OBD scan at service center; don't ignore — can be anything from loose fuel cap to serious sensor issue

Transmission Issues

  • Automatic — gear slipping or jerking: Transmission fluid check first; if fluid OK, solenoid or torque converter; workshop visit urgent
  • Manual — clutch slipping: Clutch plate wear; high-speed driving with partial clutch accelerates wear
  • CVT — vibration or noise: CVT fluid change often resolves; manufacturer-specific fluid must be used
  • AMT — jerky shifts: Often software update resolves; inherent characteristic of AMT technology at low speeds

Body, Rust & Paint

  • Rust warranty: Most manufacturers — 3 to 6 years anti-perforation rust warranty (structural rust)
  • Paint defects: Manufacturing paint warranty typically 2 years; check for chipping, peeling, fading
  • Wind noise / water leakage: Door seal / weatherstripping issue; warranty repair if within period
  • Underbody rusting in coastal areas: Not always warranty-covered; dealer negotiation may help
AI Agent Safety Protocol: For any complaint involving brakes, steering failure, or overheating — always advise the customer to stop the vehicle safely and do NOT attempt to drive further. Provide RSA helpline number immediately. Never suggest driving a vehicle with failed brakes or steering to the workshop.

EV-Specific Knowledge

Government Schemes

  • FAME II: Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of EVs — subsidy scheme (ended March 2024)
  • PM E-Drive: Successor scheme announced — Rs.10,900 Cr outlay for EV ecosystem
  • State subsidies: Maharashtra, Delhi, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu offer additional subsidies (varies)
  • EV road tax waiver: most states (Delhi, Maharashtra, UP, etc.)
  • GST on EVs: 5% (vs 28% + cess for ICE — massive advantage)

Charging Infrastructure

  • AC Home Charging: Wall box 3.3 kW to 7.2 kW; installed cost Rs.15,000–50,000 (incl. electrician); charges overnight
  • DC Fast Charging: 30–100 kW; 0–80% in 30–60 minutes; connector: CCS2 (most common), CHAdeMO
  • AC Public: Type 2 connector; slower but cheaper per unit
  • Networks: Tata Power, ChargeZone, Ather Grid, BPCL Pulse, Statiq, Ola Electric
  • Cost: Rs.12–18 per kWh at public chargers vs Rs.6–8 at home

Range & Real-World Performance

  • Tata Nexon EV: ARAI 312 km; real-world 200–250 km (city), 180–220 km (highway)
  • MG ZS EV: ARAI 461 km; real-world 350–400 km
  • Hyundai Ioniq 5: ARAI 631 km (LR); real-world 450–500 km
  • Range affecting factors: AC usage (–15%), speed (highway faster = less range), ambient temperature (cold = less range), load
  • Charge to 80% for daily use; 100% for long trips (battery longevity best at 20–80%)

Battery Warranty

  • Tata Motors: 8 years / 1,60,000 km (battery & motor)
  • Hyundai: 8 years / 1,60,000 km; battery capacity guarantee ≥70%
  • Mahindra XUV400: 8 years / 1,60,000 km
  • Battery degradation: expect 2–3% per year in Indian conditions (heat is key factor)
  • Battery replacement cost (post-warranty): Rs.4–8 Lakh depending on capacity; prices expected to fall
