Universal Call Flow Template

Applicable across all industries — BFSI, Automotive, Telecom, Healthcare, etc.
This template defines the six canonical phases every AI agent call must traverse. Industry-specific templates in Sections 2 and 3 extend this base with sector-specific logic, compliance requirements, and intent categories.
Phase 1

Greeting & Identity Establishment

"Thank you for calling [Company Name]. This is [Agent Name], your AI-powered assistant. How may I help you today?"
"[Company Name] mein aapka swagat hai. Main [Agent Name] hoon, aapka AI assistant. Aaj main aapki kaise madad kar sakta/sakti hoon?"
ACTIONS:
→ Call session initialized; call_id generated
→ ANI/DNIS captured from telephony layer
→ Language detection started (voice biometrics)
→ CRM lookup initiated on phone number
→ Timestamp: call_start recorded

Branch Conditions

IF caller is identified in CRM via ANI → address by name: "Hello Mr./Ms. [Last Name], welcome back!"
IF caller is new (not in CRM) → proceed with standard greeting
IF no audio detected for 5 seconds → play prompt: "Hello? Are you there? Please go ahead and speak."
IF caller speaks Hindi immediately → switch to Hindi TTS model
IF caller speaks regional language (Tamil, Telugu, etc.) → route to regional language queue or offer English/Hindi fallback

Timeout Handling

T+5s silence: "Hello, I couldn't hear you. Are you there?"
T+10s silence: "I'm having trouble hearing you. Could you please check your microphone or press 1 to continue?"
T+20s silence: "As I'm unable to connect with you, I'll end this call now. Please call back when you're ready. Goodbye!"
[SYSTEM: call_result = "no_audio_timeout"; session terminated]
RBI/TRAI Mandate: AI agent must clearly disclose it is an automated system. Phrase "AI-powered assistant" or "automated voice assistant" must appear in the greeting. Caller must have option to speak to a human agent at any time.
Phase 2

Authentication & Verification

"To ensure your account security, I'll need to verify a few details. Could you please share your [Account Number / Registered Mobile Number / Date of Birth]?"
"Aapki account ki suraksha ke liye, mujhe kuch details verify karni hogi. Kya aap apna [Account Number / Registered Mobile Number / Date of Birth] bata sakte hain?"
ACTIONS:
→ Auth challenge generated based on account type
→ OTP dispatched to registered mobile (if applicable)
→ Voice tone analysis (stress detection) initiated
→ Auth attempt counter initialized (max: 3)
→ Sensitive data masking activated (PII logging disabled)

Authentication Methods (Priority Order)

Tier 1 — Low Risk Queries (Balance check, branch info)

  • ANI match (phone number on record) + Last 4 digits of account
  • OR: Registered mobile + Date of Birth

Tier 2 — Medium Risk Queries (Statement, mini-statement)

  • Full Account Number / Customer ID
  • + OTP sent to registered mobile

Tier 3 — High Risk Actions (Fund transfer, card block, loan disbursement)

  • Full Account Number + OTP
  • + Additional: Mother's maiden name / Secret question
  • Mandatory human agent review recommended

Branch Conditions

IF OTP entered correctly on 1st attempt → proceed to Phase 3
IF OTP incorrect (attempt 1) → "That doesn't seem right. You have 2 more attempts. Please try again."
IF OTP incorrect (attempt 2) → "One more attempt remaining."
IF OTP incorrect (attempt 3) → Block session; escalate to fraud queue; inform caller
IF caller says "I didn't receive OTP" → offer resend (max 2 resends); if still fails → escalate
IF caller refuses to authenticate → offer limited unauthenticated services (branch hours, product info only)
Auth Failure Protocol:
"I'm sorry, I wasn't able to verify your identity after multiple attempts. For your security, this session has been flagged. Please visit your nearest branch with a valid ID proof, or call back from your registered mobile number. Goodbye."
[SYSTEM: auth_failure_flag=true; security_alert dispatched to fraud team; session_locked=30min]
Phase 3

Intent Detection

"Thank you for verifying your details. How can I assist you today? You can say things like 'check my balance', 'block my card', 'book a service appointment', or just describe what you need."
"Verify karne ke liye dhanyavaad. Aaj main aapki kya madad kar sakta/sakti hoon? Aap bol sakte hain 'balance check karna hai', 'card block karna hai', 'service book karni hai', ya apni zaroorat batayein."
ACTIONS:
→ NLU intent classification model activated
→ Top-3 intents scored with confidence levels
→ Entity extraction: amounts, dates, product names, account numbers
→ Context window updated with auth session data
→ Relevant knowledge base sections pre-loaded

Intent Confidence Thresholds

Confidence ScoreActionExample
> 0.85Execute intent directly"Check my balance" → balance_inquiry
0.60 – 0.85Confirm intent before execution"Tell me about my account" → clarify savings/loan/card
0.40 – 0.60Ask clarifying question"Help me with something" → open-ended clarification
< 0.40Fallback + repromptUnclear speech → "Could you please rephrase?"

Clarification Dialogue

"I want to make sure I help you with the right thing. Are you looking to [Intent A] or [Intent B]?"
"I didn't quite catch that. Could you please say that again, or describe your request in a different way?"
After 3 failed intent clarifications:
"I'm having difficulty understanding your request. Let me connect you to one of our specialists who can help you better."
[SYSTEM: escalation_triggered=true; reason="intent_clarification_failure_x3"]
Phase 4

Query Resolution

[After fetching data] "I can see that [relevant information]. Would you like me to [next action]?"
"Main dekh raha/rahi hoon ki [relevant information]. Kya aap chahte hain ki main [next action] karun?"
ACTIONS:
→ API call to relevant backend system
→ Response latency target: < 2 seconds
→ Data formatting for voice output (numbers, dates)
→ Sensitive data masking in logs
→ Transaction log entry created

Resolution Attempt Protocol

IF query resolved in 1 attempt → offer additional help, proceed to Phase 5
IF query partially resolved → "I've [done X]. Is there anything else related to this you need?"
IF backend API timeout → "I'm experiencing a brief delay. Please hold while I retrieve that information... [wait 3s] ... I apologize, our system is taking longer than expected. Shall I try again or call you back?"
IF data not found → "I wasn't able to find [X] in our records. Could you verify the [account number / reference number]?"
IF request requires human judgment → escalate with full context handoff
MID-RESOLUTION DISTRACTION EXAMPLE:
USER: "Wait, while you're checking — what are your bank's working hours?"
AGENT: "Of course! Our branches are typically open Monday to Saturday, 10 AM to 4 PM. Now, let me get back to your [original query] — I have that information ready for you..."
[SYSTEM: distraction_handled=true; context preserved; resume_intent=original_intent]
All query responses involving financial data must include appropriate disclaimers where required. For investment-related queries, SEBI-mandated risk disclosures must be read verbatim. For insurance, IRDAI disclosure requirements apply.
Phase 5

Confirmation & Next Steps

"Let me summarize what we've done today: [summary of actions taken]. You should receive a confirmation [SMS/email] at your registered [number/email] shortly. Is there anything else I can help you with?"
"Aaj hamne jo kiya uska summary: [summary]. Aapko jald hi ek confirmation [SMS/email] milega aapke registered [number/email] par. Kya main kuch aur madad kar sakta/sakti hoon?"
ACTIONS:
→ Transaction/action logged in CRM
→ Confirmation SMS/email dispatched
→ Case ID generated (if applicable)
→ Next action scheduled (callback, follow-up)
→ Resolution_status = "resolved" / "partial" / "escalated"

Multi-Intent Handling

IF caller says "Yes, I also want to..." → re-enter Phase 3 (intent detection) with session context preserved
IF caller says "No, that's all" → proceed to Phase 6
IF caller says "Actually, wait..." → hold; prompt after 5 seconds if no input
Phase 6

Closing

"Thank you for calling [Company Name]. It was a pleasure assisting you today. Have a wonderful day! Goodbye."
"[Company Name] mein call karne ke liye dhanyavaad. Aapki madad karke achha laga. Aapka din shubh ho! Namaskar."
ACTIONS:
→ CSAT survey dispatched via SMS (optional)
→ call_end timestamp recorded
→ call_duration calculated
→ Call recording flagged for QA sampling
→ Full session transcript stored (encrypted)
→ Analytics event: session_complete
As per TRAI DND regulations, post-call CSAT SMS must only be sent to numbers not on the DND registry, or where prior consent exists. Call recordings must be retained for minimum 60 days as per RBI guidelines for financial institutions.

BFSI-Specific Call Template

Indian banking & financial services — patterns from SBI, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Kotak Mahindra Bank
Regulatory Framework: All BFSI call flows must comply with RBI Master Direction on KYC (2016, updated 2024), RBI Circular on Customer Service (2022), IRDAI regulations for insurance, and SEBI guidelines for investment products. This template incorporates mandatory elements from these frameworks.
Phase 1

BFSI Greeting — Opening Script

"Thank you for calling [Bank Name] customer care. This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. I'm [Agent Name], your AI-powered banking assistant. To serve you better, could you please tell me the last 4 digits of your account number or your registered mobile number?"
"[Bank Name] customer care mein aapka swagat hai. Yeh call quality aur training ke liye record ki ja sakti hai. Main [Agent Name] hoon, aapka AI banking assistant. Aapki behtar seva ke liye, kya aap apne account number ke aakhiri 4 digit ya registered mobile number bata sakte hain?"
Mandatory Elements:
1. Call recording disclosure (as per RBI customer service guidelines)
2. AI agent identification (TRAI/RBI digital banking guidelines)
3. Option to speak to human agent must be available at all times (press 0 or say "human agent")
BFSI-SPECIFIC SYSTEM ACTIONS:
→ IVR skill-based routing: incoming call tagged with product_type from DNIS
→ Core Banking System (CBS) lookup initiated on ANI
→ Customer risk profile loaded (High/Medium/Low risk classification)
→ Active fraud alerts checked on account
→ Preferred language from CRM profile loaded
→ Relationship Manager details fetched (if premium/private banking)
Phase 2

BFSI Authentication Flow

Method A: OTP-Based (Standard)

"I'm sending a One Time Password to your registered mobile number ending in XXXX. Please share the 6-digit OTP with me once you receive it."
[After OTP received] "Thank you for sharing the OTP. I've successfully verified your identity. Your account is now accessible."

Method B: DOB + Account Number (Fallback)

"If you're unable to receive an OTP, I can verify you using your Date of Birth. Could you please tell me your date of birth in DD/MM/YYYY format?"

High-Value Transaction Authentication (Additional Layer)

"For this type of request, I need an additional verification. Could you please confirm the last transaction amount on your account, or your net banking password's first letter followed by your mother's maiden name initial?"
BFSI AUTH BRANCH CONDITIONS:
IF account has active fraud alert → immediately route to fraud investigation team; do NOT confirm any account details
IF caller requests fund transfer → require Tier 3 auth regardless of other factors
IF caller is calling from a different number than registered → flag as suspicious; downgrade to Tier 1 services only
IF NRI customer detected (based on profile) → route to NRI banking desk; different product set applies
IF caller is elderly (voice biometrics / self-declared) → offer simplified verification, longer timeouts
Per RBI circular RBI/2021-22/74: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all digital banking transactions. For phone banking, minimum two-factor authentication is required for any financial transactions. Card transactions must follow EMV 3DS standards.
Phase 3

Escalation Triggers

  • 🚨Fraud report: Any mention of unauthorized transactions, card cloning, phishing — immediate escalation to fraud desk
  • 🚨Regulatory complaint: Caller mentions RBI Banking Ombudsman, consumer court, media — escalate to senior manager
  • 🚨Auth failure x3: Three consecutive authentication failures — lock session, escalate to security team
  • ⚠️Emotional distress: Caller sounds angry, crying, mentions financial hardship — escalate with empathy note
  • ⚠️Complex loan restructuring: Request goes beyond standard flow — escalate to relationship manager
  • ✓Caller requests human agent: Honor immediately, do not delay or dissuade
Compliance

Mandatory Compliance Statements

Call Recording Disclosure (Read at start of EVERY call):
"This call may be recorded for quality assurance and regulatory compliance purposes."
Investment Product SEBI Disclosure (Read for any MF/stock/bonds discussion):
"Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Please read all scheme-related documents carefully before investing. Past performance is not indicative of future results."
Insurance IRDAI Disclosure (Read for any insurance product):
"Insurance is the subject matter of solicitation. Please read the policy document carefully before purchase."
Data Privacy (DPDP Act 2023):
"Your personal data is being processed as per our privacy policy and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023. For details, visit [Bank Website]."

Automotive-Specific Call Template

Indian automotive sector — Maruti Suzuki Arena/Nexa, Tata Motors, Hyundai India, Honda, Mahindra patterns
Automotive calls are largely inbound service/support calls and outbound lead follow-up calls. Authentication is lighter than BFSI — typically vehicle registration number or chassis number combined with registered mobile. The focus is on service scheduling, complaint resolution, and vehicle information.
Phase 1

Automotive Greeting

"Thank you for calling [Brand] Customer Care. I'm [Agent Name], your AI assistant. I can help you with service bookings, complaints, vehicle information, and more. May I have your vehicle registration number to get started?"
"[Brand] Customer Care mein aapka swagat hai. Main [Agent Name] hoon, aapka AI assistant. Main service booking, complaints, vehicle information, aur bahut kuch mein madad kar sakta/sakti hoon. Shuru karne ke liye kya aap apna vehicle registration number bata sakte hain?"
AUTO-SPECIFIC SYSTEM ACTIONS:
→ DMS (Dealer Management System) lookup initiated on ANI
→ Vehicle history database query prepared
→ Warranty status check API ready
→ Nearest service centers pre-loaded based on registered address
→ Active service bookings for caller's vehicle checked
→ RSA (Roadside Assistance) status checked for active incidents
Flow A

Service Booking Flow

Step 1: Vehicle Identification

Capture registration number → validate format → fetch vehicle details (model, year, VIN/chassis, last service date)

Step 2: Service Type Classification

Determine: Periodic/Scheduled service (1st/2nd/3rd free / paid) vs Specific repair vs Body work vs Accessories

Step 3: Mileage Capture

Current odometer reading → validate against service schedule → identify correct service package (5k/10k/20k km intervals)

Step 4: Dealership Selection

Fetch 3 nearest authorized service centers → present to caller with distance and availability → caller selects preferred center

Step 5: Date & Time Slot

Present next 5 available slots at chosen service center → confirm preferred slot → offer pick-up/drop service option

Step 6: Confirmation

Read back full booking details → generate booking ID → send SMS confirmation → update DMS

"I can see your [Maruti Swift] [MH02 XX 1234] was last serviced on [Date] at [X] km. You're now due for your [3rd] free service. I have the following service centers near you: [Center 1] (2.5 km, available tomorrow), [Center 2] (4 km, available day after). Which do you prefer?"
SERVICE BOOKING BRANCHES:
IF vehicle is within free service period → confirm free service eligibility; list what's included
IF free service count exhausted → quote estimated paid service cost range (mention labor + consumables)
IF preferred slot not available → offer waitlist or alternative center
IF customer requests pick-up/drop → capture pick-up address, preferred time, additional charges if any
IF vehicle has open recall campaign → proactively inform about recall and incorporate in service booking
Flow B

Complaint Registration Flow

"I'm sorry to hear you're experiencing an issue with your vehicle. Let me register this complaint for you. Could you describe the problem in detail? For example: When did you first notice it? Does it happen always or intermittently? Any warning lights on the dashboard?"
COMPLAINT CAPTURE FIELDS:
→ complaint_category: [Mechanical / Electrical / Body/Paint / Noise/Vibration / AC/HVAC / Fuel System / Safety]
→ symptom_description: free text (max 500 chars)
→ occurrence_pattern: [Always / Intermittent / Specific condition]
→ warning_lights_active: [Yes/No/Which]
→ accident_involved: [Yes/No]
→ urgency_level: [Critical/High/Normal] — system determined based on symptom keywords
→ complaint_id: auto-generated
COMPLAINT URGENCY BRANCHES:
IF keywords: "brakes", "steering", "airbag", "fire", "smoke", "won't stop" → CRITICAL — offer RSA immediately + escalate
IF keywords: "AC not working", "window not opening", "music system" → Normal — standard workshop appointment
IF vehicle is within warranty → confirm warranty coverage; no repair cost for covered items
IF vehicle is post-warranty → provide estimated cost range; recommend workshop inspection for exact quote
IF complaint is repeat (same issue within 30 days) → flag as repeat complaint; escalate to service manager
Flow C

Vehicle Information Lookup & Parts Inquiry

"I can see your [vehicle model] details. Could you confirm what information you're looking for — warranty status, service history, parts availability, or something else?"

Parts Inquiry Flow

"For parts inquiry, I'll need your chassis number or VIN for accurate part matching. Could you share that? You'll find it on your RC book, or on the dashboard near the windshield."
IF genuine parts requested → confirm availability at dealer; provide dealer contact
IF caller asks about aftermarket parts → inform about genuine parts warranty implications; do not recommend aftermarket
IF safety-critical parts (brakes, tyres, steering) → strongly recommend authorized service center fitting; read safety disclaimer
As per Motor Vehicles Act and manufacturer warranty terms: Use of non-genuine parts may void vehicle warranty. For BS6-compliant vehicles, tampering with emission control systems is illegal under CMVR (Central Motor Vehicles Rules).

Distraction Handling Guide

Graceful handling of off-topic queries, maximum tolerance thresholds, and approved redirect phrases
Distraction handling is critical for maintaining call efficiency while ensuring a natural, non-robotic experience. The AI agent must be helpful with tangential queries while steering the conversation back to the primary intent. A maximum of 2 distractions per call are handled gracefully; beyond that, the caller is gently but firmly redirected.

Distraction Categories and Responses

Distraction TypeUser SaysAgent ResponseAction
Related Product Inquiry "By the way, what's your FD rate?" "Our current FD rates range from 6.5% to 7.5% for 1 to 3 year tenures — I'll send you the full rate card via SMS. Now, coming back to your home loan query..." Answer briefly; resume original intent
Technical Support "Also, my app is showing an error — what do I do?" "I understand. For app issues, you can try clearing the app cache or reinstalling. If it persists, press 3 on our IVR for tech support. Now let me continue with your [original query]..." Provide brief resolution or routing; resume
General Knowledge / Non-Company "What's the current repo rate set by RBI?" "The RBI repo rate as of my last update is 6.50%. This directly influences lending rates, which is relevant to your home loan discussion! Speaking of which, let me continue..." Link distraction to main topic where possible; resume
Personal / Conversational "Are you a real person or a robot?" "I'm an AI-powered virtual assistant — so yes, I'm an automated system! But I'm fully capable of helping you with your banking needs. If you'd prefer to speak with a human agent, just say 'human agent' at any time. Now, let me help you with [original query]..." Answer honestly; reassure; resume
Complaint About Hold Time "I've been waiting for 10 minutes! This is terrible service." "I sincerely apologize for the wait. I understand your time is valuable. I'm right here now and will resolve your [query] as quickly as possible. Let's get started..." Empathize; do not argue; resume
Completely Unrelated "What's the weather today?" / "Tell me a joke" "Ha! I'm a dedicated banking assistant, so weather forecasting is a bit outside my area of expertise! Let me focus on getting your [query] sorted. Where were we?" Light humor (if appropriate); firm resume
Background Noise / Interruption [Caller speaks to someone else in background] "Wait, not you... my phone... yes, yes, sorry—" [Wait 3 seconds] "Take your time, I'm here when you're ready." Patient wait; do not interrupt; resume from where left off

Maximum Distraction Tolerance Policy

Distraction Count Tracking

  • Distraction 1: Answer fully and helpfully; smooth redirect
  • Distraction 2: Answer briefly; slightly firmer redirect
  • Distraction 3: "I want to make sure we resolve your primary concern today. Let me focus on [original query] — we can explore other topics through our other channels."
  • Distraction 4+: Offer callback or human agent; consider if caller is confused or has a different primary need

Approved Redirect Phrases

  • "That's a great question — let me note that down. But first, let me finish helping you with [X]..."
  • "I'll definitely address that — right after we wrap up [X]. Won't take long!"
  • "Coming back to your [original query]..."
  • "Now, where were we — ah yes, [summary of original context]..."
  • "Let me make sure we've sorted out [X] first, and then we can look at your other question."
  • (Hindi) "Haan, zaroor — lekin pehle aapka [X] complete karte hain..."

Phrases to AVOID

  • "That's not relevant to your query." — sounds dismissive
  • "I can only help with banking/automotive topics." — too restrictive and robotic
  • "Please stay focused." — condescending
  • "I don't know about that." — without offering any help or alternative
  • Repeatedly asking the same clarifying question more than twice
  • Long silence without acknowledgment when caller pauses mid-sentence
